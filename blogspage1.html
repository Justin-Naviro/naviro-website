UNDERSTANDING THE HOMELESS COMMUNITIES IN CAPE TOWN

16 FEBRUARY 2024 "Author: Marli Fraser - CFO and co-founder of Naviro"

The Reality of Homelessness in Cape Town


Cape Town's homeless community represents a diverse group of individuals facing numerous hardships. While exact figures are challenging to determine due to the transient nature of homelessness, various estimates from multiple sources suggest that thousands of people are living on the streets or in inadequate shelters across the city. 14,000 is the figure being passed around for Cape Town, with approximately 700 living in the central business district (CBD). However, it's important to note that these figures have significantly increased since 2020, due to the pandemic and its aftermath.

Causes of Homelessness


The causes of homelessness in Cape Town are complex and interconnected. Unemployment, limited education opportunities, a lack of affordable housing, social disintegration, and long-standing poverty reaching all the way back to apartheid racism, all contribute to the issue. Discrimination against poor and mentally challenged individuals, as well as gender-based inequalities further compound the problem. It is also essential to highlight the impact of migration, as individuals from other provinces or neighbouring countries often end up on the streets due to limited support networks and job prospects.

Needs of the Homeless Community


Addressing homelessness requires a comprehensive approach that goes beyond subsistence-level assistance. While providing affordable housing is crucial, it is equally important to focus on reintegrating homeless individuals into society, reuniting them with family whenever possible, and offering drug abuse treatment and mental health support. Offering educational opportunities and vocational training would equip homeless individuals with valuable skills, enhancing their employability and empowering them to break the cycle of homelessness. Furthermore, fostering a sense of belonging through community engagement initiatives can reduce social isolation, increase support networks, and promote inclusion for the homeless community.

Homeless individuals have also personally expressed a desire for safety, access to water, sanitation facilities, nutritious food, employment opportunities, and medical assistance. Additionally, it has been mentioned that assisting with obtaining identification documents can help overcome barriers to accessing essential services.

Collaborative Solutions


Addressing homelessness in Cape Town necessitates collaboration among government agencies, NGOs, community organizations, and the public. Advocacy plays a crucial role in raising awareness, challenging stigmas, and advocating for policy changes that prioritize the needs of homeless individuals. The National Homeless Network's Homeless Manifesto outlines key demands with possible solutions, including shelter, access to healthcare and social assistance, sanitation facilities, fair treatment by local authorities, and increased economic opportunities.

Naviro's Care Packages


At present, Naviro is dedicated to addressing the immediate needs of the homeless community in Cape Town by providing essential resources that can alleviate their living conditions. These fall under hygiene and sanitation, food and water, seasonal protection, and health and safety aid. While we acknowledge that this is not a comprehensive solution to the larger problem of homelessness, it is a crucial step towards building a community capable of so much more.

Our future goals encompass a holistic approach, aiming to expand our services to include rehabilitation programs that encompass education, housing, therapy, employment opportunities, community engagement, and overall health. We strive to raise awareness, challenge stigmas, foster empathy and compassion, and inspire action from other initiatives.

By working together, we can create a brighter future for the homeless community in Cape Town and contribute to the ongoing efforts of addressing homelessness on a broader scale.
